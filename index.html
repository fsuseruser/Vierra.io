01
Terminology
6 terms
Net Area (Carpet Area)
The usable floor area inside the property, excluding walls, balconies, and common areas.
Built-up Area
Carpet area plus the thickness of internal and external walls. May include balconies in some projects.
Common Area
Shared spaces — lobbies, corridors, elevators, staircases, gyms, pools, parking, and gardens.
Total Area (Super Built-up Area)
Built-up area plus the proportionate share of common areas. Typically used for pricing in most developments.
Freehold
Full ownership of the property and the land. The owner can sell, rent, or inherit without restriction.
Secondary Market
Properties resold by the current owner, rather than purchased directly from the developer.
02
Property Types
7 types + townhouse variants
Apartment
Residential unit within a multi-storey building.
Studio
One open living space with a kitchenette and bathroom — no separate bedroom.
1 / 2 / 3 BHK
Bedroom, Hall, Kitchen. A 2 BHK = 2 bedrooms, 1 hall, 1 kitchen.
Villa
Standalone house on its own plot of land.
Duplex
Two-floor apartment connected internally by a private staircase.
Penthouse
Luxury apartment on the top floor of a building, with premium views.
Loft
Open-plan home with high ceilings, often double-height — typically converted from commercial space.
Townhouse

A multi-floor home sharing one or two walls with adjacent neighbors. Comes in several variants based on plot position and layout.

04
Developers, Master Developers & Exclusive Rights
Master Developer
Plans and develops an entire community — roads, parks, infrastructure, utilities, and amenities. May invite other developers to build within the community.
↓
Developer
A company that builds and sells residential, commercial, or mixed-use properties within a community or standalone plot.
↓
Exclusive Agency
A brokerage with exclusive rights to market and sell a particular project or phase on behalf of a developer.
↓
Exclusive Agent
A single agent with the sole right to market and sell a specific property on behalf of its owner.
